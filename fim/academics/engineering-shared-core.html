What is the Common Core of Engineering?

Gives the student an opportunity to learn about the set of available careers and then take the best decision

At the beginning of 2004-2 school year the incorporation of the common core of engineering began at U.A.B.C, particularly in the Engineering Faculty.

It consists of a set of subjects that belongs to a group of related educational programs from the same area of knowledge and which are studied in the basic stage. The common core of engineering is an academic space where students enter in a basic training stage without the degree to which he or she will finally take, even if from the moment studens have an initial preference for a career in the area of interest, their initial assignment is to enter to the common core of engineering which provides them with the following benefits:

  1. It allows the attention of a greater number of students since the capacity have been increased, so the groups can be integrated when they enter to the common core of engineering, once the groups are complete and balanced, as well as the groups of each career are formed at the end of it. This stimulate the optimal use of available resources
  2. It favor students mobility since they easily start their career in one academic unit and conclude it in a different one, after accreditate their studies
  3. Promotes students basic knowledge, skills, attitudes and core values. Motivate them to solve problems and develop capacities which allow them to participate through continuous changes in different society fields, such as: sciences, technology, art, sports and culture
  4. Produces logical, critical and creative thinking
  5. Allows interpersonal and group relationships with tolerance and respect for cultural diversity
  6. Generates optimal performance based on basic knowledge and skills for permanent self-training
  7. Allows the performance of a community social service incorporated into the subjects and linked to different sectors of the entity

The common core of engineering gives the student an opportunity to learn about the set of available careers and then take the best decision. The academic units will take advantage of this space to strengthen the vocational orientation of the student and thus promote better informed decisions.

Considerations

  • 1.-To initiate the common core of engineering a tutor will be assigned. He or she will accompany during students stay in it. This tutor will recommend the critical path (career choice) according to the profesional profile in which the student shows interest.
  • 2.-Once the obligatory subjects of the common core of engineering have been accredited automatically the student must participate in the auction process to choose a career.
  • 3.-The time established to take the common core of engineering is two semesters.

Learning Units

The learning units that are taught within the common core of engineering are those shown below:

1rst Semester:

  • Diferential calculus
  • Superior Algebra
  • Programming methodology
  • Oral and written communication
  • Introduction to engineering
  • Engineer profesional development
  • English I

2nd Semester:

  • Integral calculus
  • Probability and statistics
  • Vector mechanics
  • Programming and numerical methods
  • English II

Career auction process

  • 1.-Students who have accredited the set of learning units belonging to the common core of engineering may participate in the selection at the auction process to enter into the educational program in which they would like to enroll, in any of the academic units or campus where the curriculum is offered.
  • 2.-The auction process for admission into the educational program of interest for the student will be held based on their academic merits and the allowed spaces availables in the program.
  • 3.-Studens may participate in the auction process to enter the program of their interest at any campus where the program is associated wth the common core of engineering.
  • 4.-The auction process will take place in two stages. In the first stage priority will be given to regular students who have passed the obligatory learning units of the common core of engineerin considering the general grade average obtained. In the second stage irregular students will be included.
