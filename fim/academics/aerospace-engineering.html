Aerospace Engineering Program

MISSION

To promote, study and research to reach new technologies development and provide the professional aerospace engineering community with updated knowledge, values and required skills, capable of demonstrating a high commitment to society, being capable to apply a high performance in aerospace systems development and offer specialized Aerospace Engineering services to the university community and the industry.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

VISION

In 2025, the Aerospace Engineer educational program is widely national and international recognized by prestigious evaluating organizations and their activities are developed with quality, state-of-the-art technology and a high-level teacher training, that fosters values among teachers and students promoting the development of their own technology to work on projects with productive and social sectors.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

Admission Profile

Applicants to enter the Aerospace Engineer educational program must have the following knowledge, skills, attitudes and values:

Knowledge:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Mathematics

Skills:

  • Analyze and interpret problems
  • Use of text processors, spreadsheets and presentations
  • Database use
  • Handling of materials and Laboratory equipment
  • Basic programming skills
  • Verbal and mathematical reasoning
  • Analysis and abstraction capacity
  • Verbal and written communicative skills

Attitudes:

  • Join teamwork with organization and discipline
  • Initiative and innovation
  • Interest in the use and development of Aerospace systems
  • Interest in learning
  • Professional development with competitiveness

Core Values:

  • Responsibility
  • Interest in the employment and development of aerospace systems
  • Acceptance in social relationships

Graduate Profile

Upon completion of this program, students should be able to:

  • Identify, formulate and solve complex engineering problems applying the principles of basic science and engineering.
  • Apply, analyze, and synthesize engineering design processes that result in projects that meet specified needs.
  • Develop and conduct adequate experimentation; analyze and interpret data using engineering judgment to draw conclusions.
  • Communicate effectively with different audiences.
  • Recognize their ethical and professional responsibilities in relevant situations in order to make informed judgments considering the impact of engineering solutions in a global, economic, environmental and social context.
  • Recognize the continuing need for additional knowledge and have the ability to locate, evaluate, integrate and apply this knowledge appropriately.
  • Work effectively in teams to set goals, plan tasks, meet deadlines and analyze risks.

Educational objectives

  • Contribute to the Aerospace Engineering advancement
  • Apply their knowledge and skills to the aerospace systems and components development
  • Demonstrate their leadership, ethical behavior and the ability to adapt into different environments.
  • Manage their professional development though new learning challenges.

Occupational Field

Aerospace Engineer may work in:

Private sector:

  • Government Institutions
  • Educational Institutions
  • Research Institutions
  • Parastatal industries
  • Public services

Public Sector:

  • Commercial and service companies
  • National and International Aerospace Industry
  • Educational and research Institutions

Independent professionals:

  • Advise, design, implement, document and evaluate aerospace projects
  • Design, select and install aerospace equipment
  • Maintain aerospace systems in optimal conditions
  • Generate and innovate aerospace technology
  • Commercialize and promote aerospace systems
  • Design and execute of specialized training programs

Main Projects

  1. Evaluation of properties of polymeric matrix composite materials.
  2. Aerodynamic profile optimization.
  3. Mathematical modeling for evaluation of composite materials.
  4. Component development for aerospace medicine.
  5. Manufacturing process optimization.
  6. Mathematical modeling of dynamic thermal processes.
  7. Development of aircraft prototypes and polymer matrix composite material structures.
