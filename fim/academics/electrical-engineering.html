Electrical Engineering Program

MISSION

The mission of the Electrical Engineer educational program is to comprehensively train professionals in the area of electrical engineering at the undergraduate, complying with the best educational quality standards, capable of providing optimal and sustainable solutions to problems related to the generation, transmission, distribution and utilization of electrical energy in harmony with university values and seeking both social and environmental well-being; in addition, perform basic and applied research as well as promoting technological innovation and ties with the industry.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

VISION

In 2030, the Electrical Engineering educational program is widely recognized nationally for being socially responsible, for contributing with opportunity, relevance and with the best quality standards to the comprehensive training of professionals in electrical engineering. It is an educational program accredited by prestigious evaluating bodies, both national and international. It generates, applies, disseminates and transfers knowledge, to promote both innovation and research, to strengthen ties with the industry and to offer professional services to both the university and the industry, which allows it to be an educational program of reference in Mexico and Latin America.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

Admission Profile

Applicants to enter the Electrical Engineer educational program must have the following knowledge, skills, attitudes and values:

Knowledge:

  • Algebra.
  • Trigonometry.
  • Analytic Geometry.
  • Physics.
  • Chemistry.

Skills:

  • Interpret physical phenomena from observation.
  • Logical thinking.
  • Mathematical problem solving.
  • Teamwork.
  • Verbal and written communication skills.

Attitudes:

  • Willingness for bibliographic and field research.
  • Willingness to participate in academic, scientific and cultural activities.
  • Willingness to work in teams.
  • Respect for the institution, its teachers and their peers.
  • Interest in understanding the operation of electrical systems and their associated equipment.
  • Critical, thoughtful, dynamic and transformative.
  • Creativity and imagination.

Core Values:

  • Honesty.
  • Respect.
  • Confidence.
  • Responsibility.
  • Modesty.
  • Justice.
  • Democracy.
  • Liberty.
  • Loyalty.
  • Perseverance.
  • Solidarity.

Graduate Profile

Upon completion of this program, students should be able to:

  • Identify, formulate and solve complex engineering problems applying the principles of basic science and engineering.
  • Apply, analyze, and synthesize engineering design processes that result in projects that meet specified needs.
  • Develop and conduct adequate experimentation; analyze and interpret data using engineering judgment to draw conclusions.
  • Communicate effectively with different audiences.
  • Recognize their ethical and professional responsibilities in relevant situations in order to make informed judgments considering the impact of engineering solutions in a global, economic, environmental and social context.
  • Recognize the continuing need for additional knowledge and have the ability to locate, evaluate, integrate and apply this knowledge appropriately.
  • Work effectively in teams to set goals, plan tasks, meet deadlines and analyze risks.

Educational objectives

  • Contribute to the development of public, private and service companies, related to the design, maintenance, administration and construction of high and low voltage electrical systems.
  • Promote environmental well-being by applying current regulations in the design, construction and operation of high and low voltage electrical systems.
  • Promote multidisciplinary work for a better use of electrical energy, in a rational and effective way.
  • Encourage updating and research in training processes to adapt to technological changes and advances.

Occupational Field

Graduates of Electrical Engineering may exercise their professional activity in:

Private sector:

  • Government agencies.
  • Educational institutions.
  • Research institutions.
  • Communications and transportation.
  • Public services.

Public Sector:

  • Commercial and service companies.
  • Industry and manufacturing plants.
  • Educational and research institutions.

Independent professionals:

  • Consulting and advisory activities.
  • Electrical system design, project and construction.
  • Electrical equipment control and protection system design and optimization.
  • Select and maintain electrical equipment and material in optimal conditions.
  • Electrical systems diagnosis and evaluation.

Main Projects

  1. Simulation and validation of the electromagnetic model of a transformer through the use of free software of finite elements in 2D and the development of experimental test PRODEP NPTC 2015-2 to 2016-1.
  2. Development of didactic electromagnetic models using the second order MEF-2D UABC UA 2017-1 to 2017-2.
  3. Development of a teaching system for the analysis and/or design of induction machines PRODEP CA 2017-1 to 2018-1.
  4. Development of first order EF-3D nodal software applied to electromagnetism UABC UA 2020-1 to 2020-2.
  5. Proposal and design of a technology for variable caging angle of a low capacity wind turbine for optimization of electricity generation with code UABC-PTC-526 at the call for the Program for Professional Development of Teachers, Superior Type 2015.
  6. Shortage and over-generation problems in micro networks that incorporate renewable energies with code UABC-CA-280 in the 2017 call for the Strengthening of Academic Bodies.
